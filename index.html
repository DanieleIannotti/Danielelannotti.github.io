🌱 About

I am a PhD student at Scuola Superiore Meridionale in Naples and a visiting PhD student at Universität zu Köln. My research sits at the intersection of quantum information theory, quantum many-body physics, and quantum gravity.

My overarching theme is understanding quantum complexity — how entanglement and magic (non-stabilizerness) together characterize the structure of many-body quantum states, and how symmetries, geometry, and holography constrain this complexity. I am particularly interested in the holographic interpretation of quantum resources via AdS/CFT, black holes, and the mathematical language of topology and differential geometry that underpins modern theoretical physics. If I can draw it, I understand it.

I am a team member of qmeets seminars and a quantum contributor to Physics World at IOP Publishing.
